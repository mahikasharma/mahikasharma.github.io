Hate Speech Detection (NLP)

2025 – Present

Building a hate speech detection system using the Davidson et al. Twitter dataset. The project involves implementing and comparing multiple approaches: a baseline Naive Bayes classifier, an LSTM model, and a fine-tuned BERT model. A key focus is evaluation beyond accuracy — false positive rates matter a lot when building systems that could silence legitimate speech, and hate speech datasets often encode existing biases that models can inadvertently amplify.

Bechdel Test Classification Model

2025 – Present

The Bechdel Test is a simple but widely used measure of gender representation in film: a movie passes if it has at least two named women who talk to each other about something other than a man. Despite its simplicity, a surprising number of films fail. This project builds a classification model to predict whether a movie will pass the Bechdel Test based on historical Bechdel scores from the past 20 years, then applies the trained model to recent films to see how representation has (or hasn't) shifted. Models being explored include Logistic Regression, MLP, Random Forest, and SVM.
